My work at Oak Ridge National Labs in the Summer of 2020 with Dr. Eugene Dumitrescu and at NC State with Dr. Lex Kemper is focused on efficient gate decompositions for hamiltonian simulation. This work, currently in review and available on ArXiv, adapts algorithms for universal decomposition of unitary operators into quantum gates in order to produce efficient circuit implementations of unitary time evolution operators for systems with a certain type of Hamiltonian. The method is demonstrated in comparison to Trotter based methods in optimial conditions, demonstrating that is provides a clear advantage for systems which can be modelled as non-interacting fermions on a 1D lattice. Continuing work explores the applications of this method, which is a Cartan Decomposition of the smallest Lie Algebra which contains the vector space spanned by the operators which compose the Hamiltonian,

Efekan Kökcü, Thomas Steckmann, JK Freericks, Eugene F Dumitrescu, and Alexander F Kemper, “Fixed depth hamiltonian simulation via cartan decomposition,” (2021) arXiv preprint arXiv:2104.00728

The immediate follow-up to the work with Cartan Decompsition is the implementation of the algorithm in simulating the Hubbard Model and the Anderson Impurity Model using Dynamical mean Field Theory. This work is currently in progress

The paper below, written as part of a course on the mathematics of quantum computing, focuses on algorithms for decompositing arbitrary unitary matrices into a quantum circuit. The paper focuses on the implementation of methods for generating circuits with few qubits, as all such algorithms have an exponential cost with system size.
