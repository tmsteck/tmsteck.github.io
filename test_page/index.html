About Me

Thomas Steckmann is a PhD student at the University of Maryland, College Park. Under the advising of Alexey Gorshkov and Michael Gullans in the Joint Center for Quantum Information and Computer Science (QuICS), he researches near term applications for quantum computers, error mitigation strategies, and resource optimization for quantum systems constrained by noise, hardware requirements, or error correction. This website is designed as a portfolio of current and past work in order to supplment my CV, which you can find above. During my time as an undergraduate at NC State University, I worked with Lex Kemper, Yan Wang (ORNL), and Eugene Dumitrescu (ORNL) studying algorithms for Hamiltonian simulation, fast-forwarding, and Dynamical Mean Field Theory. I also have experience with experimental research in organic semicondutors. More informaiton about these projects and others can be found in the navigation above or below.
